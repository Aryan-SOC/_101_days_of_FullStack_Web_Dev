1.Wildlife in Assam

Assam, a northeastern state of India, is renowned for its rich and diverse wildlife. The state is home to the famous Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which shelters the world’s largest population of the endangered one-horned rhinoceros. Assam's lush forests and wetlands provide habitat for an array of species, including the majestic Bengal tiger, Asian elephants, swamp deer, and hoolock gibbons. The state also boasts diverse birdlife, with rare species like the Bengal florican and white-winged wood duck. Manas National Park, another UNESCO World Heritage Site, further showcases Assam's biodiversity. These protected areas, along with numerous wildlife sanctuaries, make Assam a vital hub for conservation and a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

3.About all the National Parks of Assam

  1. Kaziranga National Park

    Located across the districts of Golaghat, Nagaon, Biswanath, and Karbi Anglong, Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its unparalleled conservation efforts for the one-horned rhinoceros. The park's sprawling grasslands, wetlands, and forests are home to diverse wildlife, including Bengal tigers, elephants, swamp deer, and wild water buffalo. Kaziranga is also a haven for birdwatchers, with numerous migratory bird species flocking here during winter. Its scenic beauty and iconic wildlife make it a major attraction for tourists and nature enthusiasts.

  2. Manas National Park

    3. Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Manas National Park spans the districts of Chirang and Baksa and is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known for its stunning landscapes of grasslands and dense forests, the park is home to the elusive Bengal tiger, the rare golden langur, and the critically endangered pygmy hog. Manas is also a part of the Manas Tiger Reserve and offers a unique blend of biodiversity and cultural heritage. Its pristine environment and rich flora and fauna make it a must-visit for wildlife lovers.

  3. Nameri National Park

    4. Situated in the Sonitpur district, Nameri National Park is known for its dense forests and the scenic Jia Bhoroli River that flows through it. The park is famous for its population of white-winged wood ducks and other rare bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. Nameri also supports elephants, leopards, and several species of deer. It offers unique activities like river rafting and jungle trekking, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in its untouched natural beauty.

  4. Dibru-Saikhowa National Park

    5. Located in the districts of Dibrugarh and Tinsukia, Dibru-Saikhowa National Park is one of the few riverine national parks in India. Known for its rich wetland ecosystem, it is a haven for feral horses and the endangered river dolphin. The park also attracts a wide variety of migratory and resident birds, making it a prominent destination for bird enthusiasts. Its serene riverine landscapes and rare wildlife provide a tranquil escape for nature lovers.

  5. Orang National Park

    6. Situated in the districts of Darrang and Sonitpur, Orang National Park is often referred to as the "Mini Kaziranga" due to its similar habitat and population of the one-horned rhinoceros. The park also hosts Bengal tigers, elephants, and numerous bird species. Its location along the northern banks of the Brahmaputra River adds to its picturesque beauty. Oranger’s efforts in preserving its unique biodiversity make it a significant contributor to wildlife conservation in Assam.
